Explore Our Solar System Planets

Mercury

Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km

Type: Terrestrial (Rocky)

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. Despite being closest to the Sun, it is not the hottest planet due to its thin atmosphere. It has extreme temperature variations, from scorching hot during the day to freezing cold at night.

Venus

Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km

Type: Terrestrial (Rocky)

Venus is Earth's sister planet in size, but it's a harsh world with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead. It has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a runaway greenhouse effect.

Earth

Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km

Type: Terrestrial (Rocky)

Our home planet is the only known world to harbor life. Its perfect distance from the Sun, protective atmosphere, and abundant water make it ideal for countless life forms. Earth is approximately 4.5 billion years old.

Mars

Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km

Type: Terrestrial (Rocky)

Mars, the Red Planet, has captured human imagination for centuries. It features the largest volcano in the solar system (Olympus Mons) and a vast canyon system (Valles Marineris). Recent discoveries suggest water once flowed on its surface, making it a prime target for the search for past microbial life.

Jupiter

Distance from Sun: 778.5 million km

Type: Gas Giant

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant so massive that over 1,300 Earths could fit inside it. Its Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth that has raged for at least 400 years. Jupiter has at least 79 known moons.

Saturn

Distance from Sun: 1.43 billion km

Type: Gas Giant

Saturn is famous for its spectacular ring system, composed of billions of particles of ice and rock. Despite being a massive gas giant, Saturn is less dense than water and would float if placed in a large body of water. It has 83 known moons, including the large moon Titan.

Uranus

Distance from Sun: 2.87 billion km

Type: Ice Giant

Uranus is unique for rotating on its side, likely due to a collision early in the solar system's history. This ice giant has a faint ring system and an atmosphere composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, which gives it its blue-green color.

Neptune

Distance from Sun: 4.50 billion km

Type: Ice Giant

Neptune is the windiest planet in the solar system, with winds reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h. It appears as a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere. Neptune has 14 known moons and a faint ring system discovered by Voyager 2.

Planetary Science Research

The study of planets has evolved dramatically with technological advancements. From ancient observations to modern spacecraft missions, we continue to discover fascinating details about each planet. Missions like Mars Rovers, the Cassini-Huygens mission to Saturn, and the New Horizons spacecraft have revolutionized our understanding of the solar system.
