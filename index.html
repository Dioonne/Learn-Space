What are exoplanets?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. They can vary greatly in size, composition, and distance from their host stars. The study of exoplanets helps scientists understand the diversity of planetary systems in the universe and the potential for life beyond Earth.

Ongoing Research in Exoplanet Studies

Scientists are actively researching exoplanets to understand their atmospheres, compositions, and potential habitability. Advanced telescopes and space missions are being developed to detect biosignatures and study the conditions on these distant worlds. The search for life beyond Earth continues to be a major focus in the field of astronomy.
